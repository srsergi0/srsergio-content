If you are here, it may be because you are afraid of code, despite the growth opportunities and increased productivity it offers.

These tools generally offer three types of service:

- Creating websites
- Automating internal processes
- Creating web or mobile MVP (Minimum Viable Product) applications

You can choose between digital tools that have developed experience in no-code, or specialized no-code tools, or autonomous experts in tools such as Webflow or Make (formerly Integromat).

## What is No-Code?

The basic principle of no-code is that tools (which are made with code) can be used to build applications or processes without writing a line of code. The fundamental logical principles of code (loop, if/else conditions) are always present, but this allows for much faster progress, especially for those who are not very familiar with it.

The main benefit is that there are no hosting and traffic management problems (SSL, redirects) and, above all, no "devOps" problems, that is, the ability to go to production.

## When to use no-code?

No code is a small revolution in the world of automation and software, but it is not always relevant. In our opinion, there are 3 cases in which it has a real advantage compared to code:

- Creating a website or e-commerce site
- Automating processes and data flows
- Creating MVPs and quick prototypes

### Creating a website or e-commerce site

Open source code has officially died in these segments. The use of solutions such as Prestashop, Magento, or even WordPress is increasingly neglected in favor of solutions such as Webflow and Shopify. The main motivation is that these sites have a constant functional perimeter, such as a product index, a cart, and a newsletter.

The no code challenge here lies in quick iterations and marketing automation (without going through marketing automation agencies). The goal is to empower marketing and product teams to avoid spending time on issues already addressed in many other places.

### Automating processes and data flows

Today, every company uses a set of software, such as marketing automation software, commercial CRM, customer support software, monitoring tools, social media management, commercial and web reporting tools, etc.

All of these solutions form a set of heterogeneous data that you will try to reconcile, although they come from different software. In general, the larger software has built integrations (native or developed by other companies) to solve this problem, accessible through an API. However, some no-code tools, such as Make or Webflow, offer a native integration that allows you to conciliate data in a simple way, without going through an API.

### Creating MVPs and quick prototypes

Finally, no-code is particularly useful for creating quick prototypes, especially for startups or digital projects. It allows you to test the value proposition of your product or service quickly and cheaply, without investing too much time and money in development. The advantage here is that you can iterate quickly and validate the product-market fit before scaling.

## Conclusion

No-code is not a replacement for traditional development. It's a complement that empowers non-technical teams and accelerates specific types of projects. The key is knowing when to use it — and when not to.
