I bought a Redmi Watch 5 and realized there's nothing to do on it. Notifications. Sleep tracking. Weather. That's it. The entire app ecosystem for wearables is empty.

So I built a full UNO game. Single-player against 3 bots. Local multiplayer passing the watch between turns. All the cards: Skip, Reverse, Draw Two, Wild, Wild Draw Four. The UNO catch rule — forget to say UNO and eat +2 cards. Pixel-art UI for the 432×514 screen. All touch-based.

The whole thing fits in a 500KB `.rpk` package. Sideload it and play. No phone needed.

Playing UNO on a watch sounds like a gimmick until you're in line at the grocery store, bored, and realize you have a full card game on your wrist. It's absurd. It's delightful. And it shouldn't be novel — every smartwatch should have games.

Built it because smartwatches are computers we wear on our wrists, and computers should be fun. The fact that this is unusual says more about the wearable industry than about the idea.
