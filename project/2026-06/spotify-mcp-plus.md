I code with music all day. But switching between my editor and Spotify to find a song, adjust volume, or skip a track breaks my focus. It's a context switch I can't afford.

I wanted my AI assistant — the same one that writes my code — to handle the music. So I could just say what I wanted and keep coding.

This MCP server connects Spotify to any AI that speaks the Model Context Protocol. Claude, Cursor, opencode, Cline. I type "play Elvis's first song" or "turn the volume down" or "create a playlist with the audio features of this track but more energetic" — and it just works.

The AI handles all the orchestration. Searching for track IDs. Chaining API calls. Interpreting audio features. I don't write commands. I don't click around. I just talk to my AI the same way I talk about code.

What makes this different from a voice assistant is context. My AI knows what I'm working on. It knows my taste. It knows that "something upbeat" at 2 PM on a Tuesday means something different than at 10 PM on a Friday. A voice assistant is a remote control. This is a collaborator.

Built it because music shouldn't be a distraction from coding. It should be part of the environment, controlled the same way you control everything else in your editor: by talking to your AI.

The protocol is the important part. MCP isn't about Spotify — it's about giving AI access to the real world. Music is just the start. The next step is everything else.
