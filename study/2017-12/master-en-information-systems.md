I came back from Hong Kong with practical experience but no solid theoretical foundation in technology management. The Master's gave me the conceptual framework to make strategic decisions, not just operational ones.

The curriculum was rigorous. IT Strategy and Governance taught me to align technology with business objectives using frameworks like COBIT and ITIL. Project Management covered PMP methodology, agile frameworks, and stakeholder management for million-dollar budgets. Data Analytics showed me how to use statistical analysis and business intelligence to drive decisions.

The capstone project was the real test. I designed a complete digital transformation strategy for a healthcare company — a three-year roadmap with budget and success metrics. Not a theoretical exercise. A real plan for a real organization.

But the most valuable part wasn't in the curriculum. It was the network. Two hundred technology professionals from over thirty countries. Perspectives I never would have encountered otherwise.

The Master's didn't teach me to code better. It taught me to think like a technology leader.
